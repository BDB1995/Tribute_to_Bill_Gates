BILL GATES

A great programmer and philanthropist

Here's a time line of Bill Gates

1955 - Born in Seattle, Washington, U.S.

1967 - Gates is enrolled to Lakeside School,an exclusive private school in Seattle, where he befriends Paul Allen. The two students share an interest in computer and programming.

1973 - He scored 1590 out of 1600 on Scholastic Aptitude Tests (SAT) and enrolled at Harvard College in the autumn of 1973. While at Harvard, he met fellow student and future Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer.

1975 - Gates made the decision to drop out of Harvard and focus on building Microsoft. He and Allen moved to Albuquerque, New Mexico, where they set up their first office and began working on their first software product, Altair BASIC.

1976 - Gates and Allen register the trademark 'Microsoft' Gates writes an open letter to computer hobbyists, condemning the early adopters for sharing, rather than paying for, software.

1987 - Bill Gates became the world's youngest-ever billionaire at the age 31 in 1987

1995 - In July, at age 39 and with a fortune of $12.9 billion, Gates becomes the world's richest man. He was the richest person in the world, according to Forbes, from 1995 through 2017 (except in 2008 and from 2010 through 2013). Largely due to the $55 billion he donated to the Gates Foundation. Later that summer in 1995, Microsoft introduces Internet Explorer to the world, as part of the Windows 95.

2000 - Gates steps down as CEO of MIcrosoft, Gates' former Harvard dorm-mate and right-hand man Steve Ballmer takes over the helm.

2006 - Gates announces that his role as the executive at Microsoft will be phased out over the following two years. His intention, he says, is the spend more time working with The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundaiton. Shortly after his announcement, Warren Buffet donates $31 billion, most of fortune, to the foundaiton.

2007 - Gates 'graduates' from Harvard, the university awards him with an honorary degree. Gates gives the commencement speech, encouraging the graduates to strive for social change. "Humanity's greatest advances are not in its discoveries," Gates says to the class, "but in how those discovers are applied to reduce inequality"

2010 - Bill and Melinda challenged the global health community to declare this the Decade of vaccines. They pledge $10 billion over the next 10 years to help research, develop, and deliver vaccines for the world's poorest countries.

2013 - Bill heled launch a $5.5 billion effort to eradicate polio by 2018. India was certified polio-free by the World Health Organizaiton, leaving only three countries that have never been free of the diseases.
